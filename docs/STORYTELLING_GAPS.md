# Storytelling Gaps — Skyways Timeline

## Context

The timeline tells the WHAT of Skyways well — contracts, dates, aircraft versions, customer wins. But a timeline that makes investors act and the wider public care needs to also tell the WHY, the WHO, and the WHAT-NEXT. This doc lists what's missing, ranked by impact.

Audiences:
- **Investors** want de-risked signals: capital efficiency, moat, PoR pathway, pipeline, leadership.
- **Wider public** wants story: human origin, mission impact, characters, why this matters for the world.

Both audiences get served by the same timeline if the right beats are there.

## The Five P0 Gaps, Expanded

Each of these is worth a dedicated content block on the site, not just a tweaked line.

### 1. Capital efficiency story
$46M+ in government contracts vs. ~$17M raised across Series A (including all follow-ons). That's a 2.7x non-dilutive-to-equity ratio. Few defense startups have that profile at this stage. A single visual callout ("For every $1 raised from investors, Skyways has earned $2.70 from the government") would reframe the funding story.

### 2. Founder origin (the hobby kit)
Charles's Apr 1 DM has this verbatim: "V1 was a Skywalker X-5 hobby kit. I added 2 carbon fiber booms and installed VTOL propulsion. There was no CAD until we started V2.1." Turning this into a timeline beat, or even a footnote on the 2016 card, converts the apartment line from cute to specific. It's the defining origin image Skyways has and isn't using.

### 3. Mission-impact framing
Project ULTRA delivered temperature-controlled blood 20 flights between two US airports. What isn't on the timeline: the first time Skyways blood reached a patient, the operational lag saved, the use case for contested-logistics trauma care in Pacific theater. Each mission has a human impact the copy doesn't name. The ANA Okinawa medical trials have the same gap.

### 4. Program of Record explanation
A 1-sentence sidebar or glossary entry: "A Program of Record is a formally funded, multi-year DoD procurement line item — the structural mechanism for sustained defense revenue, as opposed to one-off contracts." Without this, "pathway to PoR" reads as jargon to half the audience. The $37M STRATFI means nothing without the PoR context.

### 5. Competitive moat / market positioning
A single block that names: (a) incumbent alternative (heritage rotorcraft — Sikorsky/Bell, 1/30th the cost line), (b) direct competitors (Zipline in medical delivery, PteroDynamics at RIMPAC, Matternet/Drone Delivery Canada in civil), (c) Skyways moat (range, payload, VTOL, autonomy, government-integrated path). Without this, "autonomous air network" sounds like every other drone pitch.
